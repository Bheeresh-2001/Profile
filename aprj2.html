Home Automaton Using Brain Signal

INTRODUCTION

Main aim of this project is to help paralyzed and physically disabled people to control the home appliances using Electroencephalogram (EEG) signal, so they become independent in their daily life.
The BCI is considered as a recent and unique transmission medium between the human brain and a computer.
We know that a physically handicapped would be in short of a physical or sensory functionality. This is deteriorating the interaction or no interaction at all with theirsurroundings. Thus, leading to considerable inconvenience. Activities of normal people prove to be near impossible feats to them. A simple action of switching on a light would be challenging for such individuals. Two methods of obtaining EEG signals for a BCI exist: invasive and non-invasive EEG. In the non-invasive approach we just use an external sensor for acquiring the brainwave signals. In the invasive EEG method, signals are captured by electrodes surgically implanted within the depths of the brain.
A BCI is an AI arrangement that can identify a definite set of arrangements of brain signals in further five consecutive stages: signal acquisition, preprocessing or signal enhancement, feature extraction, classification, and the control interface. Non-invasive methodologies have successfully been used by severely and partially paralyzed patients to reacquire basic forms of communication and to control neuroprosthetics and wheelchairs. EEG signals are easily procured in a non-invasive manner through electrodes placed on the scalp, for which that reason it is by far the most widespread recording modality. This can be implemented further for car automation, door automation and even in aircrafts. Here only attention level of the brain wave is taken for controlling of appliances. In future all the other waves can be used for better results.

BLOCK DIAGRAM

The above signals are analysed by BioAMP EXG Pill the signal contain some noises, which are filtered using, the filter present in BioAMP EXG Pill. The filtered signal of EEG Signal/Brain Signal consists of Alpha, Beta, Theta and Delta which is a Raw Data. This Raw Data is transmited through Bluetooth Transmitter. The transmitter Raw Data is collected using Bluetooth Receiver. The received data is analysed and they are seperated based on the frequency. If the frequency matches then the respective Home appliances are made ON, like (For Alpha Signal LED Blub, For Beta Signal Fan …,) Now we are taking the EEG Signal data and giving this data as input to the Matlab and getting the respective signal. The Matlab and Arduino are interfaced and signals are feed into Arduino, again based on the signals respective Home appliance are made to ON.

REFERENCE ELECTRODE

The Head Band is use to collect the EEG signal that is generated from the brain and Process the signal using TGAM Module and transmit the Processed signal to Arduino Uno via Bluetooth. The reference Electrode is clipped to the Ear. Although the ear clip can be attached anywhere on the ear, literature suggests, that the outer portion of the ear is the best placement option, since there is no nearby brain activity.

MAIN ELECTRODE

The brain signal is captured using Dry Electrode we are making use of Dry Electrode instead of Wet Electrode because as the gel which is applied dries the accuracy and efficiency drops so we are making use of Dry Electrode
